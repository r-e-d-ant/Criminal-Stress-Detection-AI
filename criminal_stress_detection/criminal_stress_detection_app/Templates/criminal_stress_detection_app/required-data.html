Required Data

Being able to detect whether someone is stressed or not require to have some data about the person, for our AI we need six inputs

Respiration rate

Stress triggers the release of stress hormones, such as adrenaline, which can lead to a faster and shallower breathing pattern. This increased respiration rate is part of the body's "fight-or-flight" response.

Body temperature

During periods of acute stress, the body's "fight-or-flight" response is activated, leading to the release of stress hormones such as cortisol. These hormones can elevate body temperature as part of the body's physiological response to stress.

Limb movement

Stress often leads to increased muscle tension and stiffness, which can restrict normal limb movement and range of motion.

Blood oxygen

During stressful situations, individuals may engage in shallow or rapid breathing, leading to a decrease in the depth of each breath. This can limit the amount of oxygen intake and reduce blood oxygen levels.

Eye movement

Stress can lead to increased eye movement, including rapid and frequent saccades (quick shifts in gaze). This can be observed as the eyes scanning or darting around, indicating heightened arousal or anxiety.

Heart rate

Stress can cause fluctuations in heart rate, leading to irregular beats or palpitations. This can be a result of increased sympathetic nervous system activity and altered electrical signaling in the heart.
